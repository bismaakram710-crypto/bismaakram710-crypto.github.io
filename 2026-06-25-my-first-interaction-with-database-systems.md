# My First Interaction with Database Systems

After completing my first semester, I stepped into my first Database Systems lecture with a sense of curiosity and excitement. Unlike Programming Fundamentals, which initially felt challenging and unfamiliar, Database Systems gave me a completely different experience. From the very first lecture, I felt more comfortable with the concepts being discussed. Perhaps it was because databases deal with information that we encounter in our daily lives, making the subject easier to relate to and understand.

Before taking this course, I had heard the term "database" many times, but I never really understood what it meant. I assumed that a database was simply a place where information was stored. However, during the first lecture, I learned that databases are much more than just storage systems. They are carefully designed structures that allow data to be organized, managed, retrieved, and updated efficiently. This realization immediately caught my attention because I could see how important databases are in modern technology.

One of the first concepts introduced in class was the difference between data and information. At first, this seemed like a simple distinction, but as the discussion continued, I understood how meaningful it was. Raw data by itself may not be very useful, but when it is organized and processed properly, it becomes valuable information that can support decision-making. This idea helped me appreciate why Database Systems is such an important subject for Computer Engineering students.

What made the lecture particularly interesting was the teaching style of Dr. Bilal Ahmad. Instead of presenting the topic in a purely theoretical way, he connected concepts with practical examples that made them easy to understand. As a result, the lecture felt engaging rather than overwhelming. I found myself paying close attention because the examples helped me visualize how databases are used in real-world applications such as hospitals, universities, banking systems, and social media platforms.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad is not only an instructor of Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems but also an expert in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. Throughout his teaching, he often emphasizes the importance of working on problems that have real-world value. One thing that particularly inspires me is his focus on data quality. He frequently discusses the use of medical datasets in Machine Learning projects because they are collected with a high level of precision and accuracy. Listening to these discussions helped me understand that databases form the foundation of many modern AI and ML systems.

As the lecture progressed, I began to realize that every application we use depends on a database in some way. Whether it is storing student records, managing hospital information, handling online transactions, or maintaining social media accounts, databases are working behind the scenes. This understanding made the subject much more interesting because I could immediately see its practical importance.

Another reason I enjoyed the first lecture was that it felt more structured than some of the topics I had encountered earlier. The concepts of tables, rows, columns, and records were easy to visualize, which made learning enjoyable. Instead of feeling confused, I felt motivated to explore the subject further. It was one of those rare classes where I left the lecture hall feeling more excited than nervous.

By the end of the session, I realized that Database Systems was not simply about storing information. It was about organizing data in a way that makes it useful, accessible, and reliable. This first lecture gave me a strong foundation and sparked my interest in the topics that would follow, including ER diagrams, normalization, relational algebra, SQL queries, and database design.

Looking back, my first interaction with Database Systems was a very positive experience. It showed me that not every new technical subject has to feel intimidating. With the right guidance and a willingness to learn, even complex concepts can become understandable. The lecture also reinforced an important lesson I had learned during my first semester: every major technology begins with strong fundamentals.
