During my second semester, our classes shifted from traditional classrooms to online learning. This change brought both challenges and opportunities for students.

At first, adapting to virtual lectures was difficult. Managing time, staying focused during online classes, and dealing with internet connectivity issues required extra effort. However, over time, I learned how to organize my study schedule and make effective use of digital learning platforms.

Online learning also helped me develop independent learning skills. I became more comfortable using educational tools, submitting assignments electronically, and collaborating with classmates through virtual meetings.

Although I missed face-to-face interaction with teachers and friends, the experience taught me flexibility and self-discipline. It showed me that learning can continue successfully even outside a traditional classroom environment.

Looking back, the transition to online classes was an important part of my academic journey and helped me become a more adaptable learner.
