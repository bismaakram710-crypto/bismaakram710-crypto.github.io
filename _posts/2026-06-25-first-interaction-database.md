After completing my first semester, I attended my first Database Systems lecture with curiosity and excitement. Compared to Programming Fundamentals, this subject felt easier to understand from the beginning because it focused on organizing and managing information that we use in everyday life.

Before this course, I thought a database was simply a place where data was stored. During the first lecture, I learned that databases are much more than storage systems. They help organize, manage, retrieve, and update information efficiently. I was particularly interested in learning the difference between data and information and understanding how databases support real-world applications such as hospitals, universities, banking systems, and social media platforms.

One reason I enjoyed the lecture was the teaching style of Dr. Bilal Ahmad. He explained concepts using practical examples, making them easy to understand and relate to real-life situations. His approach kept the class engaging and helped me quickly grasp the importance of Database Systems.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad is also an expert in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. He often discusses how high-quality data, especially medical datasets, plays a crucial role in building effective AI and ML models. These discussions helped me realize that databases form the foundation of many modern technologies.

By the end of the lecture, I felt motivated and confident. It was a positive start to the semester and sparked my interest in learning more about database design, ER diagrams, normalization, and SQL.
