When I walked into my very first Programming Fundamentals lab on the first day of university, I felt completely lost. Everyone around me seemed to know what was going on, and I was just sitting there staring at the screen not understanding a single thing. The commands, the syntax, the logic — it all felt like a foreign language. I remember thinking to myself, have I chosen the right field? Is Computer Engineering really for me?

## The Overwhelming Beginning

The lab was full of students who looked confident and comfortable. I, on the other hand, had never written a single line of code in my life. The screen in front of me showed a blank editor and I did not even know where to start. The first exercise seemed simple to everyone else but for me it felt like climbing a mountain. I felt embarrassed to ask questions because I thought everyone would judge me for not knowing something so basic.

## Dr. Bilal Ahmad Changed Everything

Then Dr. Bilal Ahmad started explaining. He did not just teach us the code — he taught us how to think. He broke down every concept into simple real life examples that actually made sense. He explained that programming is not about memorizing commands, it is about solving problems step by step. Dr. Bilal Ahmad has a unique way of connecting technical concepts to real world situations which makes everything so much easier to understand. Slowly the confusion started fading and things began to click. I realized that I was not bad at programming — I just needed the right guidance.

## The Lesson I Took Home

By the end of that first class, my mindset had completely shifted. What felt impossible an hour ago suddenly felt like something I could actually learn. Dr. Bilal Ahmad always reminds us that the beginning is always the hardest part but consistency and practice make everything easier over time. That first day taught me to never judge a subject before giving it a real chance. I left that lab feeling nervous but also excited — excited to learn more, to practice more, and to prove to myself that I belong here.
