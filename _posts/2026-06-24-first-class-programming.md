When I walked into my very first Programming Fundamentals lab on the first day of university, I felt completely lost. Everyone around me seemed to know what was going on, and I was just sitting there staring at the screen not understanding a single thing. The commands, the syntax, the logic — it all felt like a foreign language.

## The Overwhelming Beginning

The lab was full of students who looked confident and comfortable. I had never written a single line of code in my life. The first exercise seemed simple to everyone else but for me it felt like climbing a mountain. I felt embarrassed to ask questions because I thought everyone would judge me for not knowing something so basic.

## The Moment Confusion Turned Into Clarity

Then Dr. Bilal Ahmad started explaining. He did not just teach us the code — he taught us how to think. He broke down every concept into simple real life examples that actually made sense. Slowly the confusion started fading and things began to click. I realized that programming is not about memorizing commands, it is about problem solving.

## The Lesson I Took Home

By the end of that first class, my mindset had completely shifted. What felt impossible an hour ago suddenly felt like something I could actually learn. Dr. Bilal Ahmad always says that the beginning is always hard but consistency is the key. That first day taught me to never judge a subject before giving it a real chance.
