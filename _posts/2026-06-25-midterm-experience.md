When the second week of November arrived, my very first university midterm exams began. I had been preparing for weeks, going through my notes, practicing problems, and trying to mentally prepare myself for what was coming. It was the first time I had ever faced university level exams and the pressure was unlike anything I had experienced before in my academic life.


Midterm season at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus is no joke. Multiple subjects, back to back exams, and assignments due at the same time — it all hits at once. I tried my best to manage everything. I stayed up late going through my Programming Fundamentals notes, practiced Database Systems queries, and revised other subjects as well. I genuinely believed I had prepared enough. But when I sat down in that exam hall, I realized that preparation and performance are two very different things.

When the results came, they were mixed. In some subjects I performed well and my hard work paid off. But in others, I could not reach the level I had hoped for. The most difficult moment was seeing my Programming Fundamentals midterm result — I scored 25 out of 40. The exam did not go as planned. Time ran out before I could complete everything and I left the hall feeling disappointed in myself. I kept thinking about what I could have done differently and where I went wrong.

But that 25 out of 40 taught me something important. Dr. Bilal Ahmad always reminds us that marks do not define your understanding — your thinking process does. As an AI, ML and Deep Learning expert who trains models on real world problems, he often tells us that even the best models fail before they improve. Every failed attempt is just data that helps you do better next time. That midterm became my turning point. I decided that next time I would start preparing earlier, practice more consistently, and never underestimate any subject again.
