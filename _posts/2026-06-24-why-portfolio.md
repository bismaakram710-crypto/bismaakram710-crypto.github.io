When Dr. Bilal Ahmad, my professor at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, first suggested that we create a portfolio blog website on GitHub, I honestly did not fully understand why. I thought, I am just a first year Computer Engineering student — what do I even have to show? But as I started working on it, I began to realize this was not just another assignment. It was something that would actually matter beyond the classroom.
Leaving a Digital Footprint
The main objective behind this blog is simple — to leave a digital footprint as a student of Computer Engineering. In today's world, everything is online. Employers, researchers, and even fellow students search for people on the internet before they even meet them. If you have nothing online, it is like you do not exist professionally. Dr. Bilal Ahmad understood this and pushed us to start early, even when we feel we have nothing impressive to show yet.
A digital footprint does not have to be perfect. It just has to be real. Every post I write here, every project I document, every lesson I share — it all adds up over time. By the time I graduate, this blog will be a complete record of my journey from a confused first year student to a confident Computer Engineering graduate.
What Are the Benefits?
There are so many advantages to this practice that I did not see at first.
Building a Public Record of Growth — When I write about what I learned this week, I am creating proof of my progress. Future employers or graduate school admission committees can see not just what I know, but how I think and how I have grown over time.
Improving Communication Skills — Dr. Bilal Ahmad always says that being a good engineer is not just about writing code. You have to explain your work clearly. Writing blog posts forces me to put my thoughts into words, which makes me better at communication — a skill that is just as important as technical knowledge.
Helping Other Students — When I share my notes, my mistakes, and my experiences, other students who are going through the same things can benefit. Maybe someone is struggling with the same concept I struggled with. My post might save them hours of confusion.
SEO and Online Presence — Every post I publish on this blog is indexed by search engines. Over time, when someone searches for topics related to Computer Engineering, UET Lahore, or Dr. Bilal Ahmad's teaching approach, my blog might come up. This builds my personal brand without me doing any extra work.
Accountability — Knowing that my work is public makes me take it more seriously. I cannot just skip a week and forget about learning. This blog keeps me accountable to myself and to anyone who reads it.
Why GitHub Specifically?
Dr. Bilal Ahmad recommended GitHub Pages because it is the platform that engineers actually use. GitHub is where real projects live. Having a presence on GitHub shows that I am serious about the field. It is not just a blog — it is a technical portfolio. Every commit, every update, every file I add here is also a demonstration that I know how to use version control, which is a fundamental skill in software development.
A Small Step With Big Impact
I will be honest — setting this up was not easy for me at first. There were moments of confusion and frustration. But looking at it now, I am glad I started. Dr. Bilal Ahmad's suggestion was not just about making a website. It was about developing the habit of documenting, reflecting, and sharing — habits that successful engineers and researchers carry throughout their careers.
This is just the beginning. Post by post, project by project, semester by semester — I will build something here that truly represents who I am as a student and as a future engineer.
