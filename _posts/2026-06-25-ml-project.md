Every semester has that one assignment that feels bigger than everything else. For me, that assignment was our Machine Learning project. It was the moment when everything we had been learning in theory had to be applied in practice. No more just reading about models and algorithms — it was time to actually build one. And honestly, the experience was one of the most memorable things I have done so far in my Computer Engineering journey at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus.

## Forming Groups and Choosing a Dataset

The first step was forming project groups. Dr. Bilal Ahmad organized us into pairs — two students per group — which I think was a great decision. Working with a partner means you can divide the work, discuss ideas, and push each other when things get difficult. It also teaches you how to collaborate, which is one of the most important skills in any engineering field.

Once the groups were formed, Dr. Bilal Ahmad provided us with a collection of datasets to choose from. This is one of the things that makes his teaching approach so unique — he does not just assign a generic task. He gives students real datasets and encourages them to work with data that has actual meaning. He often emphasizes medical datasets in his own research because of their high precision and accuracy, but for this project, students had the freedom to choose what interested them. You can learn more about his research and work on his [LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/).

My group chose the TMDB Movies dataset. TMDB stands for The Movie Database, and the dataset contains detailed information about thousands of movies — including genres, ratings, release dates, budgets, and revenues. It was a dataset we found genuinely interesting, and we believed it had enough depth to build a meaningful Machine Learning model around it.

## Training the Model

After selecting our dataset, the real work began. We had to clean the data, explore it, identify patterns, and then train a Machine Learning model on it. This process taught me so much more than any lecture could on its own. I understood firsthand why data preparation is so important — messy or incomplete data leads to poor model performance, no matter how good your algorithm is.

We spent hours going through the dataset, removing missing values, selecting the right features, and deciding what our model should predict. Every step required discussion and decision making. There were moments of frustration when things did not work as expected, and moments of excitement when something finally clicked. That back and forth between confusion and clarity is something I have come to see as a normal and necessary part of the learning process.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad guided us throughout. His expertise in AI, ML, and Deep Learning meant that whenever we were stuck, we had access to someone who truly understood the subject at a deep level. He did not just give us answers — he asked us questions that pushed us to think through the problems ourselves. Working under his guidance made the entire experience richer and more meaningful.

## The Presentation Day

When presentation day arrived, I felt a mix of excitement and nervousness. We had worked hard on the project and I wanted to do it justice. Standing in front of the class and presenting our work was a completely different experience from sitting at a desk and writing code. Suddenly we had to explain what we did, why we did it, and what our results meant — all in a clear and confident way.

I will be honest — we were a little confused at the start of our presentation. The nerves got to us and there were moments where we stumbled over our explanations. We were not sure how to present certain technical decisions we had made, and that uncertainty showed. But as the presentation continued, something shifted. We found our rhythm, our confidence grew, and we started explaining our work more clearly and naturally.

By the end, we had walked the class through our entire process — from choosing the TMDB dataset, to cleaning and preparing the data, to training our Machine Learning model and analyzing the results. The questions that came from the audience also helped us realize how much we actually knew. Answering those questions on the spot showed us that we had understood the project far better than we had given ourselves credit for.

## What the Project Taught Me

This ML project was one of the most valuable learning experiences of my first semester. It taught me that Machine Learning is not just a theoretical subject — it is a practical tool that can be applied to real data to find real insights. It also taught me the importance of teamwork, communication, and the ability to explain technical work clearly to others.

The experience of being nervous at the start of the presentation and then finding confidence as it went on was itself a life lesson. It reminded me that the beginning of something difficult is always the hardest part. Once you push through the initial fear, things become much more manageable.

Most importantly, this project showed me what Dr. Bilal Ahmad has always emphasized — that real learning happens when you apply knowledge to real problems. Reading about ML models is one thing. Actually building one, training it on a real dataset, evaluating its performance, and presenting your findings to an audience is something else entirely. That project gave me a taste of what real engineering work looks like, and it made me more excited than ever about the journey ahead.
