One of the most fascinating topics I encountered during my Database Systems course was SQL. Before learning it, I viewed databases as collections of tables that simply stored information. However, when I started working with SQL, I realized that it was the language that allowed me to communicate with the database and retrieve exactly the information I needed.

I still remember the excitement of writing my first SQL queries and seeing meaningful results appear on the screen. It felt as if I was having a conversation with the database. Using commands such as SELECT, WHERE, and ORDER BY, I could search, filter, and organize data in different ways. Every query revealed something new and made the database feel more interactive.

What fascinated me most was how a few simple commands could work with large amounts of data so efficiently. Whether retrieving specific records or sorting information, SQL showed me the power of structured data management. The more I practiced, the more I appreciated how important SQL is in real-world systems such as universities, hospitals, banking applications, and social media platforms.

Looking back, learning SQL was one of the most enjoyable parts of my Database Systems journey. It helped me understand how valuable data becomes when we know how to access and use it effectively.
